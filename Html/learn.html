What are ducks anyways

A duck is a common name for various species of aquatic birds in the family Anatidae, known for their relatively small size, shorter necks, and large bills compared to swans and geese. Key characteristics include webbed feet for swimming, dense, waterproof feathers that keep their downy underlayer dry, and an omnivorous diet. Ducks are highly adaptable, found globally in freshwater and saltwater, and are often social animals living in groups.

Where do ducks sleep?

Ducks sleep on water because it offers safety from land predators and provides a quick escape route if threatened. Water-based sleeping also keeps them close to food sources in their aquatic habitats.

What is a special fact about ducks?

Ducks, in particular, have been domesticated to be exploited for their feathers, eggs and meat. All ducks have highly waterproof feathers. A duck's feathers are so waterproof that even when the duck dives underwater, the downy underlayer of feathers right next to the skin will stay completely dry.
